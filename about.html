Philosophy

Design is a decision-making process, not a deliverable.

Good design is invisible. It solves the right problem in the simplest way, and it holds up under pressure — under edge cases, accessibility requirements, engineering constraints, and real user behaviour that doesn't match the happy path. I've learned that the most important skill in product design is knowing when to kill an idea you love.

I work best when I'm close to the problem — close to users, close to data, close to engineers. The further design drifts from those inputs, the more beautiful and useless it becomes.
